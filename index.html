History of Greek Gods

The Greek gods are part of one of the most influential mythological systems in history. Originating from ancient Greece, they represented natural elements, human traits, and abstract forces. Led by Zeus on Mount Olympus, these deities played roles in countless myths explaining the origins of the world, the nature of humanity, and the laws of the universe. Stories of their triumphs, flaws, and interactions with mortals continue to inspire literature, art, and culture to this day.
